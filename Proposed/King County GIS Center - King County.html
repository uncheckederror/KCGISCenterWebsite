King County GIS Center

We put geographic information to work for King County and beyond

🗺️ Maps and Apps

💾 GIS Data

🍎 Spatial Services and Training


The King County GIS Center  manages the King County Geographic Information System (KCGIS), King County’s cooperative, multi-department, enterprise GIS. We provide efficient, high-quality GIS leadership, coordination, infrastructure, and services to meet the business needs of our customers and clients in King County government, the Puget Sound region and beyond.

Questions or concerns? Email us at giscenter@kingcounty.gov.
Mailbox monitored Monday through Friday, 8am–5pm.

The King County GIS Center is located on the 2nd floor of King Street Center in Seattle's historic Pioneer Square neighborhood. We're next door to King Street Station which provides Sounder commuter rail and Amtrak connections, one block from the Seattle Streetcar Occidental Mall Station, two blocks from the International District/Chinatown Station for the Downtown Seattle Transit Tunnel, within a short walk of numerous bus routes and stops, and just over a half mile from the Washington State Ferries, King County Water Taxi, and Kitsap Fast Ferries terminals.
